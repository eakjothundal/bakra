## Design Context

### Users
Close family and friends of Eakjot, invited to his Bakra Party (May 19). The "hosts" are his two dogs, Abel and Astro, who cordially invite guests to "watch their dad get goated." Audience is tight-knit — people who will immediately get the joke. 98% will be on a phone, likely tapped through a WhatsApp or iMessage link. First impression needs to land fast and feel special, not like a generic event page.

### Brand Personality
**Quirky, fun, festive.**

Tone: irreverent warmth — the kind of invite that makes you snort-laugh and then feel genuinely excited. The dogs are hosting. A goat is involved. There's a Flappy Bird mini-game. Lean into the absurdity without losing craft. Think hand-painted carnival signage, South Asian festival energy, and a dash of old-west cheekiness.

### Aesthetic Direction
The current direction is locked and working well: **dark western + South Asian gold warmth.**
- Dark warm brown backgrounds (`#1a1410`), gold (`#d4a017`), rust/terracotta (`#8b3a1f`)
- Rye display font for headlines (western serif with personality)
- Grain/noise overlay for that vintage parchment feel
- Animated medallions, shimmer effects, star scatters

Open to refinements that add more visual personality — more texture, more festivity, more delight — without abandoning the established palette and font choices.

**Anti-references:** generic event apps (Evite, Partiful), anything that looks like a SaaS landing page, clean minimal tech aesthetics, light mode.

### Design Principles
1. **Phone-first, thumb-friendly** — everything critical lives in the lower 60% of the screen; tap targets are generous; no pinch-to-zoom needed anywhere.
2. **Delight over information** — this is a party invite, not a form. Every screen should have at least one moment that makes someone smile before they read the details.
3. **Committed absurdity** — the dogs-as-hosts premise works only if fully committed. Lean in, don't hedge.
4. **Festive texture over flat surfaces** — grain, gold shimmer, hatching, worn edges. The app should feel like it was printed on something real.
5. **Fast, snappy interactions** — transitions under 300ms, immediate tap feedback. Guests are impatient; reward their curiosity instantly.
